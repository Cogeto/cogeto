## When you may need a commercial license

The AGPL's obligations — in particular the requirement to release the complete corresponding source of your modified version to everyone who interacts with it over a network — are not workable for every organization. You should talk to us about a commercial license if any of the following apply:

- You want to **incorporate Cogeto (or parts of it) into a proprietary or closed‑source product** and distribute or offer it without releasing your source.
- You want to **offer Cogeto as a hosted or managed service to third parties** under your own branding, without meeting the AGPL's network‑source obligation.
- Your organization has a **policy that prohibits the use of AGPL‑licensed software**, or your legal or procurement team cannot accept AGPL terms.
- You want to **combine Cogeto with proprietary code** in a way that AGPL copyleft would otherwise reach.
- You need **contractual assurances** (for example warranties, indemnities, or a defined support relationship) that an open‑source license does not provide.

If you are self‑hosting Cogeto internally and are comfortable meeting the AGPL obligations, you do **not** need a commercial license.

## What a commercial license does — and does not — cover
